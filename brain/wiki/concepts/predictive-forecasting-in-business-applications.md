## Predictive Forecasting in Business Applications

Predictive forecasting uses historical and current business data, often powered by [[concepts/ai-in-erp]], to estimate future demand, trends, risks, and operational needs.

## Core idea

In business applications, forecasting is not just reporting what happened. It is an AI-enabled planning capability that helps teams anticipate what may happen next and act earlier. It can support demand planning, inventory decisions, cash-flow outlooks, staffing, sales planning, and risk management.

## Why it matters

- Helps businesses move from reactive operations to proactive planning.
- Uses ERP data such as sales history, purchase patterns, customer behavior, and financial records.
- Can improve inventory planning by estimating future demand and reducing stockouts or overstock.
- Can support financial planning by forecasting revenue, expenses, and cash needs.
- Can help teams identify trends earlier and adjust strategy before problems become visible in manual reports.

- Forecasting product demand from sales trends.
- Estimating future inventory needs from purchase and sales history.
- Predicting seasonal changes in revenue or workload.
- Identifying unusual demand patterns that may require planning action.
- Supporting management decisions with forward-looking business signals.
