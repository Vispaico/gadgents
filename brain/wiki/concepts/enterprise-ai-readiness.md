# Enterprise AI Readiness

Enterprise AI readiness is the degree to which an organization can turn AI into practical, measurable business impact across departments. It is not only a technology question; it also depends on departmental priorities, data availability, process maturity, governance, and willingness to adopt AI in everyday work.

## Core idea

Enterprise AI readiness is best understood as a department-by-department assessment. Different teams may be ready at different speeds because they vary in data access, process standardization, customer contact, automation potential, and leadership support. This makes [[concepts/ai-adoption-by-department]] relevant to the concept.

## Why readiness matters

An organization becomes AI-ready when it can connect AI use cases to real operational or business outcomes. Readiness includes having clear goals, reliable data, accountable owners, governance for risk, and a way to measure success. The source post points directly to this outcome by asking which department can create measurable business impact with AI today, connecting the concept to [[concepts/measurable-ai-business-impact]].

## Practical questions for organizations

- Which department has the clearest AI use cases?
- Where is the data already available and usable?
- Which teams can measure impact quickly?
- Which functions face urgent business pressure to improve efficiency, quality, or customer outcomes?
- What governance is needed before scaling AI beyond pilots?
